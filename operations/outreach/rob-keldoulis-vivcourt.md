# Letter to Rob Keldoulis — VivCourt

**Recipient:** Rob Keldoulis, Founder, Vivienne Court Trading (ex-Optiver Australia founder)
**Channel:** Physical letter, handwritten envelope, standard stamp
**Enclosures:** One-page info sheet (Posit), embossed notebook with inside-cover dedication, private Loom link (3–4 min, face-in-corner, no music)
**Date drafted:** 2026-04-16

Rob,

I've worked as an options trader at Citadel Securities my entire career, and inside it I watched one of the most instructive transitions of my working life.

When I started as a grad, the desk was siloed. Each trader had their own allocation of symbols, individual PnL was tracked, and bonuses followed. Internal competition was rife. Communication was stifled — people feared that voicing an idea would hand a colleague a slice of their own pie. Then the firm moved to a team model: a shared book, a shared PnL, a shared outcome. The room opened up. People talked. The work got better, and so did the numbers.

But even inside that more collaborative world, something kept breaking. Not every opinion got voiced. We'd stumble over each other — one trader putting on a position another was quietly reversing. Opinions expressed only in conversation were ephemeral; they faded, and the most recent voice carried the most weight by default. And when three or four people on a desk held overlapping but distinct views on the same name, there was no honest way to reconcile them, even with the best intent.

The missing piece, I think, is a common language. A way to express a positional view in terms precise enough that it cannot be forgotten, cannot be quietly overwritten, and composes cleanly with every other view on the desk. Mathematical, not verbal. Written down, not remembered.

I am building that common language. It's called Posit — a formalisation of positional trading, where every trader's view is declared as a structured signal with explicit parameters: magnitude, confidence, time horizon, and how it composes with every other view on the desk. Three traders holding distinct views on the same name don't collide — their views compose, weighted by confidence, according to agreed-upon rules. Nothing gets lost. Nothing gets silently reversed. The desk's collective conviction is visible to anyone who walks up to the screen.

I'd like VivCourt to be the first desk to use it. Free of charge, for as long as it's useful to you. I'll be a forward-deployed engineer — in your office several days a week, building what your traders actually need, iterating against real positions rather than my own assumptions.

This idea was born at Citadel, and it was gently discouraged there. I'm writing to you because the profit motive inside titans like Citadel and Optiver has quietly rewritten what gets rewarded — individual bonus optimisation, at the cost of the craft itself and of everything trading could be in service of. You named that drift before most people were willing to, and you've spent the years since showing that a trading firm can be a source of real good in the world, not just a machine for extracting it. You left to build something different. That's exactly why I'd rather build this with you than anywhere else.

The envelope includes a one-page brief on the platform and a link to a short demo. My contact details are at the end.

The trading world still has room to grow. I admire what you've already done to move it forward. I'd be honoured to take the next step with you.

With thanks,
